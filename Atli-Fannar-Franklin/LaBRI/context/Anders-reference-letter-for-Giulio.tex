{\bf Recommendation for Giulio Cerbai}
\bigskip
\bigskip

Dear Professor Wallner,

I am writing in support of Giulio Cerbai's application for the
postdoctoral position in the UNPAC project at TU Wien. I have known
Giulio since 2018 and have worked with him continuously since then.
He was a postdoctoral researcher in my group at the University of
Iceland from 2022 until 2025. Giulio is a very
productive and technically versatile combinatorialist, and I have
enjoyed our collaboration immensely.

Giulio obtained his PhD from the University of Florence in 2021,
supervised by Luca Ferrari, with a dissertation on sorting
permutations with pattern-avoiding machines. In the five years since,
his research has broadened substantially. His publication list now
includes close to twenty papers appearing in or accepted by journals
such as the Journal of Combinatorial Theory Series A, Advances in
Applied Mathematics, the European Journal of Combinatorics, Discrete
Mathematics, the Electronic Journal of Combinatorics, Annals of
Combinatorics, and the Proceedings of the Edinburgh Mathematical
Society. Beyond his original research on pattern-avoiding stacks, he has
developed a substantial programme on ascent sequences and Fishburn
structures, on pattern transport theorems, and on Cayley permutations,
a class of words generalising permutations. Their study has grown into
a research area of its own, and Giulio has been instrumental in that
growth.

Several aspects of Giulio's work connect directly to UNPAC. For instance, our joint
paper on counting fixed-point-free Cayley permutations establishes that the
proportion of fixed-point-free Cayley permutations tends to $1/e$,
mirroring the classical phenomenon for permutations and for
endofunctions. The proof goes through combinatorial species and
generating functions. His joint work with Bruce Sagan on modified
difference ascent sequences and Fishburn structures, and our papers on
Caylerian polynomials, similarly make heavy use of generating-function
methods and bivariate enumeration. Giulio is a specialist in enumerative
combinatorics with a good grasp of analytic combinatorics. He has the
mathematical maturity to take up the complex-analytic and probabilistic
threads of the project quickly.

Programming is a strength of Giulio's. He writes Python and SageMath
fluently and uses the computer as a laboratory: when we start a new
project he typically arrives with a well-tested conjecture, and often
with a candidate bijection or functional equation suggested by the
data. I should also mention that he taught a combinatorics course at the
University of Iceland in the autumn of 2023, using combinatorial species
as the organising framework; the students responded well to his clarity
and patience.

On a personal level, Giulio is a pleasure to work with. He is
independent, diligent, and open to criticism; he generates research
problems of his own rather than waiting to be handed them; and he is
generous with collaborators. He organised the NORCOM25 conference and
co-organised our departmental math seminar during his time in Iceland.

In short, Giulio would be an excellent postdoc for the UNPAC project
and for the Combinatorics and Algorithms group at TU Wien. His
application has my strongest support. Please do not hesitate to
contact me if there is anything further I can add.

Yours sincerely,\medskip\\
