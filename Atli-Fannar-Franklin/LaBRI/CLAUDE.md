## Atli's profile

Background: anders has supervised Atli since his BSc; was his PhD
advisor at the University of Iceland (PhD April 2026).

Education: BSc (double, math + CS, U. Iceland), MSc (pure math, U.
Zurich), PhD (U. Iceland, 2026). Sigurður Helgason award as
undergraduate. BSc project gave the first combinatorial proof of the
continued fraction expansion for the Fubini-number generating function.

Thesis: `Enumerating score sequences and permutations by inversions
and forbidden patterns`, four papers.

- Paper I (PAMS 2023, joint): settles Hanna's conjecture on
  tournament score sequences via a factorization theorem; closed
  formula and quadratic-time algorithm.
- Paper II (EJC 2023, joint): generating functions for the diagonal
  and subdiagonals of the Mahonian triangle.
- Paper III (DMTCS 2024, single-author): pattern-avoiding
  indecomposable permutations enumerated by inversions, for all sets
  of classical patterns of length ≤ 3.
- Paper IV (submitted 2025, single-author): encodes 1324-avoiding
  permutations as walks in directed graphs; would give a new lower
  bound of 10.418 on the growth rate (conditional on a conjecture).

Non-thesis: a Taxman paper in Discrete Applied Mathematics.

Other activities: long teaching record at U. Iceland and U. Reykjavik
(competitive programming, mathematical programming, C++, linear
algebra, graph theory, formal languages, etc.); IMO team leader,
deputy, jury member; problem author for math and programming
competitions.
